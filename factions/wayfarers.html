The Wayfarers' Brotherhood

“We walk not alone, nor for power — but for those who’ve lost their way.”

The Wayfarers' Brotherhood is a decentralized yet coordinated group of survivors united not by ideology or science, but by a shared goal: to survive, adapt, and build a future inside the Backrooms.

Where /\sync Corporation brings technology, bureaucracy, and secrets, the Brotherhood brings compassion, protection, and a deep knowledge of the levels gained through hard-earned experience. These two factions now find themselves at odds, with tension mounting into skirmishes near critical access points and storage zones.

Purpose and Philosophy

  • Survival First: The Brotherhood focuses on gathering resources, mapping levels, and sheltering wanderers.
  • Freedom from Control: Unlike corporate factions, they believe no group should dominate or exploit others within the Backrooms.
  • Shared Strength: Every member is encouraged to contribute — either through skills, knowledge, or effort. No one is left behind.

Structure

While not governed by rigid hierarchy, the Brotherhood operates through local Circles — independent camps and enclaves spread across multiple levels. Each Circle elects a Pathfinder, who represents their interests in wider decisions.

  • Pathfinders: Veteran explorers who coordinate missions and represent their Circles.
  • Keepers: Protectors of camps and supply routes.
  • Hearthhands: Medics, cooks, and technicians who sustain the Brotherhood’s people day to day.

Conflict with /\sync

The Brotherhood and /\sync are increasingly at odds. While /\sync seeks to research, exploit, and perhaps even weaponize the Backrooms under the guise of control, the Wayfarers see this place as a new frontier — dangerous, yes, but also sacred in its mystery.

In multiple documented cases, /\sync agents have attempted to take over Brotherhood enclaves or claim mapped sectors as “experimental zones.” These events are slowly escalating into cold hostility.

Outposts and Activity

Wayfarers Brotherhood have a base in level 0 with advanced construction.

Final Notes

"If we are to endure, we must do so together — not through conquest, but kinship."
